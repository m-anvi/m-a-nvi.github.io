Hello!
I am a high school senior at Issaquah High School in Washington state. My current plan is to attend a four-year university and eventually pursue a doctorate degree. As a student, I seek every possible opportunity to expand my knowledge on astronomy, neuroscience, and scientific research methods and advocacy. In college I plan to study cognitive science. I hope to contribute to the advancement of science by ultimately becoming an astronaut. It soudns like a childhood dream, and it is in a way, but going to space is a genuine professional goal that I take very seriously.


Why cognitive science?
Following my undergraduate studies, I aim to attend graduate school and achieve a doctoral degree. My ultimate career goal is to become either an astronaut or a flight director. Space travel takes an immense, often over-looked, amount of mental strength, and the stress involved with space exploration (and similar high-stakes operations) can be greatly alleviated by advanced technology. By studying cognitive science, interactions between people and increasingly common computers have the potential to be improved. My fascination with the powerful and beautiful machine that is the human brain, combined with my experience in robotics and astronomy contributes to my excitement about the future of artificial intelligence and highly capable robots. I am highly interested in participating in research and internships to gain valuable experience to supplement my education and enable me to learn as much as I can while immersing myself in a rich, vibrant community of the scientists and scholars who walk IUâ€™s halls. From the core curriculum to the work done by the Cognitive Science Lab, I see my career and education goals supported at IU. My motivation to investigate human consciousness and worldview stems from my own mental illnesses. In 2015 I was diagnosed with clinical depression/anxiety. For months on end I felt weak and helpless, which put severe tensions on my academic and social lives. Between medications, therapy, and spiritual treatment, the one thing that gave me the strength to push through and rise above my illness was the pursuit of knowledge regarding the human mind, in a mission to better understand myself. Along this journey, I became captivated by the study of consciousness and am eager to learn all that I can so that I may integrate it with my other interests to ultimately contribute to the exploration of the world within and around us.
